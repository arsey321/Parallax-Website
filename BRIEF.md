# Website Design & Development Brief: Parallax Parfums

## 1. Executive Summary

Parallax Parfums is a bespoke, independent fragrance house based in Santa Clara, California. Founded by an engineer, the brand operates at the intersection of technical precision and artisanal craft. We specialize in modern, high-concentration fragrances (Parfum/Extrait) designed to offer exceptional value and all-day performance.

Currently, our business model relies exclusively on local, direct-to-consumer sales via farmers markets and pop-up events in the Silicon Valley/Bay Area. We do not currently ship products.

The primary goals of this website are:

- Establish a world-class, luxury digital presence that rivals established niche houses.
- Educate the consumer on the "Parallax" duality concept (Umbra vs. Lucent).
- Drive local foot traffic to our physical pop-ups and events.
- Capture email leads to build an audience for a future e-commerce launch.

## 2. The Core Concept: "Parallax"

**The Definition:** Parallax is the visual effect where the position or direction of an object appears to differ when viewed from two different angles or lines of sight.

**The Translation:** This dictates our entire brand architecture. We explore scent from two diametrically opposed perspectives. The website must visually and experientially reflect this duality.

## 3. Brand Architecture & Visual Identity

The developer should utilize our established packaging aesthetics to inform the UI/UX.

- **The Master Brand (The Datum):** Precision, engineering, structure. The overarching brand color is a sophisticated Slate Grey, accented by Cashmere Taupe.
- **The Umbra Reserve (The Shadows):** Dark, light-absorbing, heavy, and resinous. Visually represented by charred wood textures, matte black glass, and deep copper accents.
- **The Lucent Reserve (The Light):** Bright, light-reflecting, transparent, and sheer. Visually represented by raw undyed linen, frosted glass, and polished silver accents.

**Logo:** The "Dynamic Circle Shift" master logo (vector files will be provided).

## 4. Required User Experience (UX) & Design Direction

- **The "Parallax Scroll" Effect:** Given the brand name, the developer should employ highly refined, subtle parallax scrolling effects (where background images move at a different speed than foreground text) to create a sense of depth and shifting perspectives.
- **Split UX / Duality Navigation:** The UI should immediately introduce the user to the two collections. Consider a homepage split-screen or a dark-mode/light-mode toggle that allows users to physically shift the website's aesthetic depending on which collection they are viewing.
- **High-End Minimalism:** Clean typography, ample negative space, and a focus on high-resolution product photography and 3D renders.

## 5. Site Architecture & Content Requirements

The site should be a concise, easily navigable brochure/showroom.

- **Home:** A striking hero section introducing the Parallax concept. Clear pathways to explore Umbra and Lucent. A prominent section highlighting the next local pop-up date.
- **The Story (The Engineer's Approach):** A page dedicated to the founder's transition from engineering to perfumery. Highlight the technical rigor behind the high-concentration Parfums.
- **The Collections (Catalogue Mode):** Detailed pages for Umbra Reserve and Lucent Reserve. Include fragrance pyramids (Top, Mid, Base notes) and the emotional intent of each scent. **Crucial:** Instead of an "Add to Cart" button, use a "Where to Experience" button that anchors to the events calendar.
- **Where to Find Us (Events Calendar):** A dynamic, easily updatable list or map of upcoming farmers markets, artisan fairs, and pop-ups in Santa Clara and the surrounding Bay Area.
- **Newsletter Sign-Up:** A persistent footer or subtle pop-up inviting users to "Join the Reserve" to be notified of local events and our future online launch.

## 6. Technical Requirements

- **Future-Proof CMS:** Even though we are not selling online right now, the site should be built on a robust e-commerce platform (like Shopify) in "Catalogue Mode." This allows for seamless Point-of-Sale (POS) integration at physical markets now, and a flip-of-a-switch launch for online shipping later.
- **Local SEO:** The architecture must be optimized for local search queries (e.g., "niche perfume Santa Clara," "local fragrance house Bay Area").
- **Performance:** The site must load instantly, particularly on mobile, as users will likely be looking us up while walking around a farmers market.
